2me: Preventing Newborn Falls

2me is an innovative baby bassinet that allows new mothers in the hospital to easily, safely, and painlessly transfer their baby to and from the crib, using a cantilever design and tilting mechanism. The Hospital of University of Pennsylvania: Mother and Baby Unit is experiencing an increase of newborn falls due to a variety of reasons. UNESCO’s Baby Friendly Initiative is becoming more prevalent in hospitals in the country. This initiative encourages the newborn to stay in the hospital room with the mother 24 hours a day, promotes breastfeeding, and discourages newborns to stay in the nursery while mothers get rest. For this reason, 69% of all newborn falls have resulted from mothers falling asleep with the baby in her arms from lack of sleep or the release of oxytocin from breastfeeding. Our mission is to design a product that encourages mothers to be close with her baby, while ensuring that baby’s safety.
